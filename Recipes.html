Nigerian Jollof Rice

Description:

Jollof Rice, a classic Nigerian rice dish,
flavoured with tomatoes, pepper and
lots of seasonings!

Ingredients

-5 medium sized Roma tomatoes,
roughly chopped
-1 red ball pepper,roughly chopped
-1 medium sized onion,roughly
chopped set aside
-2 scotch bonnet pepper(habanero
peppers as they are sometimes
called)
-1/2 cup of groundnut oil
-3 tbsp tomato paste
-2 cups of parboiled rice
-2.5 cups of chicken stock
-1 tsp salt to taste
-1/2 tsp curry powder
-1/2 tsp thyme
-1 tsp all purpose seasoning
-1 knorr stock cube
-2 bay leaves
-Water as needed

Directions

Step 1
+ Blend your tomatoes,scotch bonnet peppers and red pepper in
blender for about 45 seconds, make sure that everything is blended
well.

+Wah your rice and parboil it,do make sure it is not too
musty.

+In a medium sized pot, heat your oil
on a medium -high heat.Once the oil is
heated add the onions you set aside
and fry just until they turn golden
brown



Step 2
+Once the onions,have turned brown
in color,add the tomato paste and fry for 2-3 minutes.

+Then add the blended tomato
mixture(reserve about 16 cup and set
aside) and fry the mixture with the
onions and tomato paste for about
30 minutes. Make sure you stir
consistently so that the tomato
mixture does not burn.

+ After 30 minutes, burn the heat down
to medium, and add the chicken
stock. Mix and add your seasonings
(salt,curry powder, thyme,
purpose seasoning,and the knorr
stock cube). Continue to boil for 30
minutes.

Step 3
+Add the parboiled rice to the pot.Mix
it very well with the tomato stew.At
this point if you need to add water so
that the rice is level with the tomato
mixture/chicken stock go ahead and
do so.Add the bay leaves, cover the
pot, and cook on medium to low heat for 15-30 minutes.

+When the liquid has almost dried up
add the remaining tomato stew,
cover,and let it cook for another 5-10
minutes heat until the liquid has completely dried up.

+Turn off the heat, mix thoroughly and your Jollof Rice is ready to be eaten!
